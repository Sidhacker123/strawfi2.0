Executive Summary

NVIDIA has once again reasserted its AI leadership with Q1 FY2026 results that exceeded revenue expectations despite geopolitical headwinds. Record data center sales underscore insatiable demand for AI compute, while the company faces temporary revenue risk from U.S. export controls to China. Long-term fundamentals remain intact, underpinned by superior FCF generation, global AI buildouts, and sustained software ecosystem dominance.

We value NVIDIA using a robust DCF model and estimate its fair value per share to be in the $160–$180 range with long-term intrinsic value approaching $687/share under aggressive FCF growth assumptions.

Competitive Landscape

AMD

  • Launch of MI300X accelerators aimed at high-performance AI workloads
  • Gaining traction with select hyperscalers but lacks software ecosystem equivalent to CUDA
  • Market share remains <15% in AI training compute

Intel

  • Focused on CPUs and IFS. Minimal share in AI accelerators
  • Emerging foundry business may support custom AI chip design but not near-term competitive

Chinese AI Chipmakers (e.g., Biren, Huawei Ascend)

  • Targeting domestic AI buildouts as U.S. sanctions block NVIDIA
  • Long-term competitive risk if restrictions persist, but lag behind in software and tooling

Strategic Growth Drivers

  • Product Innovation: Launch of Blackwell Ultra and NVIDIA Dynamo platform
  • Cloud AI-as-a-Service: Partnerships with AWS, GCP, Azure enable DGX Cloud scale
  • Geographic Diversification: Expansion into sovereign AI infrastructure in UAE, Saudi Arabia

Risks to Thesis

  • Export Controls: Immediate ~$8B impact from blocked H20 sales to China
  • Supply Chain: HBM memory supply constraints could delay shipments
  • Customer Concentration: 5 clients make up ~50% of data center revenue
  • Valuation Volatility: High multiple may increase downside if sentiment shifts

Conclusion

NVIDIA's combination of cutting-edge GPU hardware, deep software integration, and global AI infrastructure scale places it in a class of its own. Despite political frictions and high expectations, fundamentals remain exceptional. With sustainable FCF generation and a global runway for growth, we reiterate a 12-month fair value target of $160 while recognizing a long-term intrinsic upside toward $600+ per share.
